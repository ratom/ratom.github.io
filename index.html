About Me

I am a PhD graduate at Charles Sturt University (CSU), Australia, specialising in deep learning, computer vision, and large language models. My research focuses on cattle identification using muzzle biometrics, with contributions spanning novel neural architectures, few-shot learning, and scalable data pipelines for agricultural and health applications.

I am currently working as a Research Associate at CSU, leading applied AI projects in wound tissue segmentation, 3D cattle reconstruction, and workplace bullying detection using LLMs with agentic AI frameworks. I also mentor PhD students and serve on a PhD supervisory team.

My work sits at the intersection of academic research and real-world deployment — I have hands-on experience building production ML pipelines on cloud platforms, deploying deep learning models, and collaborating with industry partners including Integrity Systems and the Australian Computer Society. Code is hosted in private repositories due to ongoing IP agreements; demonstrations are available on request.

PhD Research Contributions

GTE-YOLOv5 — Automated Muzzle Detection
Ghost Transformer Encoder · You Only Look Once v5
Proposed GTE-YOLOv5 integrating Ghost Convolution (−19% parameters) and a Transformer Encoder for enhanced spatial feature understanding, achieving ~10% accuracy improvement over existing models on CSU farm data. A scalable, efficient solution for livestock biometric identification.
MHAFF — Multi-Head Attention Feature Fusion
CNN + Transformer · Adaptive Feature Integration
Developed MHAFF to adaptively fuse local CNN features and global Transformer features, outperforming traditional fusion methods. Grad-CAM visualisations confirmed accurate highlighting of discriminative muzzle regions, with superior accuracy and generalisation using fewer training epochs.
CoCoMAML — Few-Shot Cattle Identification
Cooperative Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning
Introduced a novel CoCoMAML framework enabling cattle identification from as few as 5 images per animal. Using MHAFF as the feature extractor, the system adapts rapidly to new cattle identities without extensive retraining — enabling scalable real-world herd management.
